CRIME★PARTNER

Nightlife Campaign Series × 3 New York City — 2025 Organizer · Visual Designer · Experience Designer

Overview

CrimePartner is a recurring nightlife meetup series that blends anime, cosplay, music, themed games, and social interaction. Across three events, the project tested how fandom culture could reshape nightlife into a more approachable and participatory social environment.

For many young audiences, nightlife can feel socially intimidating, especially arriving alone or in small groups. Anime, cosplay, and the CP (犯罪搭档) framing gave people a shared identity and a reason to engage with strangers. Each edition was iterated based on post-event surveys and real-time feedback.

The DJ setlist was rebuilt each edition after audience feedback. By edition 3, the VJ integrated anime clip projections with the DJ performance, and the random dance segment expanded to include K-pop challenges and trending audio, meeting the audience where they actually are.

Audience & Participation Model

Core Fandom Participants
Familiar with anime, cosplay, CP culture, and character-based social play. Came to perform their identity and find community.
Casual Participants
Lightly familiar with anime culture, attracted by novelty, music, and atmosphere. Needed low-barrier entry points to engage.
Game-Oriented Participants
Interested in game-centered formats. Audience surveys mentioned League of Legends, Stardew Valley, and social gaming.
Dance & Music Participants
Familiar with K-pop challenges, Douyin trends, and viral audio. The random dance section was built for this group.
Guests / DJ / VJ / Host
Live roles that needed stage timing, visual assets, event information, and real-time coordination.
Design Challenge
Make nightlife feel social and easy to enter for fandom communities, without depending on hardcore anime knowledge as the barrier.

Feedback Loop

50+ post-event surveys and RedNote comment analysis drove every format decision between editions. The iteration process was systematic: collect → read signal → adjust format → test live.

Collect
Post-event surveys and RedNote comments gathered data on DJ playlist, alcohol options, game ideas, theme preference, and atmosphere.
Read Signal
Identified which formats felt too niche, too passive, or too hard to join, and where participation friction was highest.
Adjust Format
Expanded random dance, tuned DJ/VJ direction, reduced format complexity, and built Game Night from survey data.

Impact & Reflection

Impact

Three recurring nightlife events, ~130 attendees per event. Developed from a theme-based anime party into a standardized nightlife format supported by campaign planning, audience feedback, guest promotion, programming design, and live operations.

Reflection

CrimePartner taught me that recurring event design is not about a strong theme alone. It's about building a flexible system that responds to audience behavior. Each event became a test for the next one: comments, dance responses, complaints, and requests all became signals for the next iteration.
