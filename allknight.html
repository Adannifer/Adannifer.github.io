01 — AllKnight NYC

ALL★KNIGHT

Anime & Cosplay Convention DUMBO, Brooklyn — 2025 Organizer · Visual Designer · Experience Designer

Overview

AllKnight NYC was a large-scale anime and cosplay convention built from the ground up for New York's young fandom community, developed over two months, with promotion, vendor outreach, NPC recruitment, visual production, and on-site experience planning running in parallel. I led the project end-to-end — from concept and pre-production planning to visual system design and on-site coordination.

The audience wasn't just looking for a convention to attend. They wanted to cosplay, meet people with shared interests, interact with characters, play games, take photos, and feel recognized within a fandom-based social environment. This shifted the project from a passive event model into an immersive, participatory community experience.

Venue: a 4,000+ sq ft elementary school in DUMBO, Brooklyn, five minutes from the subway, fully furnished. Nine NPC game stations, six vendor booths, a main stage for live performances and random dance, and a dedicated cosplay photo area.

Community Brief

NYC's young anime fandom had online communities but no dedicated offline space to meet, cosplay, compete, and recognize each other in person. A passive "come and watch" convention model wasn't enough. The audience wanted to perform their identity and be seen by others who shared it.

Cosplayers

Wanted a venue to wear their costumes, interact with NPCs, and be photographed in a dedicated environment.

Fandom Participants

Wanted social entry points: game stations, interactive NPCs, and shared activities that made meeting strangers natural.

Vendors

Needed the right audience: anime and cosplay buyers in a curated, fandom-adjacent market context.

NPCs

Performers who inhabited characters and ran game stations. Needed briefing, scheduling, and on-site coordination.

General Attendees

New to conventions. Needed clear wayfinding, a stage program, and enough activity density to stay engaged.

Design Response

Shift the event from passive entertainment to an immersive, participatory community system with multiple simultaneous activity layers.

Event Design

I designed and programmed the full event layout across five distinct zones running in parallel, ensuring there was always something to do regardless of what stage the program was at.

9 NPC Game StationsCharacter-based interactive stations. Each NPC was briefed with a character identity, game mechanic, and visual role.
6 Vendor BoothsCurated anime and cosplay market: merchandise, prints, accessories. Positioned to complement the NPC circuit.
Main StageLive performances and random dance. Stage flow was scripted with transition cues and host prompts.
Cosplay Photo AreaDedicated backdrop and lighting setup for attendee photography, turning cosplayers into content creators on-site.
WayfindingDesigned location posters, event signage, and staff routing system that directed 150+ attendees through a multi-zone school building.
Staff SystemBuilt and briefed a 10-person staff team with assigned zone coverage, timing, and escalation protocol.
NPC SchedulingRecruited, briefed, and scheduled 11 NPC performers across the floor, managing character rotations and break timing on-site.
Vendor OnboardingLed vendor outreach, curation, and setup coordination, producing floor map and onboarding materials over the 2-month production period.

Impact & Reflection

Impact

AllKnight NYC demonstrated that a niche fandom audience, with the right spatial programming, social logic, and visual identity, could fill a 4,000+ sq ft venue with 150+ attendees and sustain engagement across multiple simultaneous activity zones for a full day.

Reflection

Building from zero meant every system, from vendor curation and NPC briefing to stage flow, social content, and wayfinding, had to be designed and coordinated simultaneously. The main lesson: immersive events work when the spatial design, the social rituals, and the brand identity all reinforce the same idea of who belongs there.
