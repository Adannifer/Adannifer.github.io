03 — Canton Fair × Yuanfang

CANTON★FAIR

Retail Brand Activation Canton, China — 2025 Design Intern · Brand Experience & Event Marketing

Overview

Yuanfang is a food retail platform with a franchise-oriented business model. For the Canton Fair, the brand needed to communicate its platform identity, vendor network, food offerings, and offline store concept within a crowded, high-traffic fair environment.

The fair served multiple audience groups simultaneously: potential franchise partners evaluating business opportunities, individual entrepreneurs interested in the product, and general visitors exploring the floor. The design system had to speak to all three, creating a coherent brand presence that functioned as both retail environment and franchise pitch.

Responsibilities spanned pre-opening booth setup, store interior concept curation (product display layout, flower wall installation, signage placement), live broadcast graphics, and on-site visitor guidance through the Yuanfang sample store, where 80% of visitors were prospective franchise leads.

Strategy Brief

The Canton Fair is the world's largest trade fair, running 13 days with 20,000+ visitors across categories. Yuanfang's franchise food platform needed to communicate its brand to three fundamentally different audience types, simultaneously, within a high-traffic, visually competitive floor environment.

Franchise Partners
Evaluating the Yuanfang business model as an investment opportunity. Needed to see platform credibility, scale, and ROI framing at a glance.
Individual Entrepreneurs
Interested in a single product or starting a small operation. Needed approachable pricing and a clear product story.
General Visitors
Exploring the fair floor without a specific intent. Needed visual appeal, sampling opportunities, and guidance into the booth.
Challenge
Design a single brand presence that simultaneously functions as a retail environment, franchise pitch, and visitor experience, without losing coherence across all three.
Context
80+ vendor booths on the Yuanfang floor. The sample store was the primary conversion environment: 80% of sample-store visitors were prospective franchise leads.
My Role
Design Intern covering booth setup, store interior curation, live broadcast graphics, product display layout, signage placement, and on-site visitor guidance.

Space & Identity Design

The brand presence spanned two distinct environments: the main vendor floor and the Yuanfang sample store, each requiring different design priorities and audience communication logic.

Fair floor overview: 80+ vendor booths across the Yuanfang footprint. Signage and visual hierarchy had to work at distance before visitors stopped.

Sample store exterior: designed to draw visitors off the main floor and into a conversion-optimized interior environment.

Product display: organized by category and franchise relevance, with franchise-facing SKUs prominently featured.

Product profile system: each featured product included visual identity, origin story, and franchise positioning.

Brand Assets & Live Broadcast

Visual production extended beyond static signage into live-format content: the fair's live broadcast stream required real-time graphic support that maintained brand consistency while adapting to a dynamic on-floor environment.

Booth SignagePrint and display materials communicating platform identity, vendor categories, and franchise credentials at the booth level.
Sample Store InteriorProduct display layout, flower wall installation, and signage placement designed to guide visitors from browsing to conversion.
Live Broadcast GraphicsOn-screen overlays, titles, and product spotlights produced for the fair's live streaming coverage of the Yuanfang floor.
Visitor Guidance SystemPhysical wayfinding from the main floor into the sample store, using directional cues, staff positioning, and threshold design.

13-Day Sprint

The fair ran for 13 consecutive days. Design and operations ran in parallel: assets were produced or adapted on-site as the event evolved and visitor patterns became clear.

Pre-Open
Booth Setup & Store Dressing

Pre-opening day: booth assembly, product display layout, flower wall installation, and signage placement across all zones.

Days 1–3
Visitor Flow Observation

Monitored which signage and display elements drew visitors in and which product profiles generated franchise inquiry. Adjusted placement accordingly.

Days 4–8
Live Broadcast Production

On-site graphic support for live streaming coverage: real-time product spotlights, brand overlays, and floor highlights.

Days 9–13
Sample Store Conversion

Full focus on visitor guidance through the sample store. 80% of store visitors identified as prospective franchise leads by event close.

Impact & Reflection

Impact

80+ vendor booths, 20,000+ fair visitors, and a sample store where 80% of visitors were prospective franchise partners. The design system successfully translated a franchise food platform into a coherent brand presence across three distinct audience contexts (retail, pitch, and general discovery) within a single physical environment.

Reflection

The Canton Fair taught me that design at scale means designing for many simultaneous audiences, not one. The most critical skill wasn't creating standout visuals. It was understanding the conversion logic of each audience type and building a spatial and visual system that could speak to all three without contradiction.
